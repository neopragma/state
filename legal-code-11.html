Households

11. Households.
This section of the legal code concerns the definition, registration, and dissolution of households, and matters of individuals joining or withdrawing from households.

11.1. A household is a registered group of consenting adults which functions as a unit for general purposes of life.

11.2. For all administrative and legal matters not listed in this statute, the individual is the party who makes decisions, takes actions, and holds responsibility. For matters in the areas listed below, the household may be treated as the party who makes decisions, takes actions, and holds responsibility:

  • Probate law
  • Visitation rights
  • Child-rearing
  • Tax-supported benefits

11.3. When an individual belongs to a household, statutory laws and administrative procedures of the government pertaining to the areas listed in Statute 11.1 will apply first to the household, and secondly to the individual.

11.4. Membership in a household is not mandatory for any legal or administrative purpose.

11.5. Any group of two (2) to four (4) consenting adult Citizens and/or Residents may register as a household by following a simple administrative procedure.

11.6. The government is authorized to charge a fee to offset the cost of processing the registration of a household. It is the intent of the law that any such fee be trivially small, and not a barrier to anyone registering a household.

11.7. Any individual adult may join a registered household with the approval of the current members of the household. The specific definition of "the approval of" is at the discretion of the members of the household.

11.8. No one may belong to more than one household at the same time.

11.9. Any children under the care of an adult who joins a household automatically become members of that household.

11.10. Any adult member of a registered household may withdraw from that household by following a simple administrative procedure.

11.11. A child in the care of an adult who withdraws from a household will remain in the care of that adult, provided

  • there is no objection by any other member of the household, and:
    • the child was in the care of the same adult at the time the adult joined the household, or
    • the child reached human status during the time the adult belonged to the household, and
      • the child is the result of pregnancy or cloning of the adult, or
      • the remaining members of the household agree to the arrangement,
    and
  • the adult has not been declared an incompetent or unfit parent.

In all other cases, the care of the child will be determined by

  • guidance by social services to achieve agreement of the parties; failing that,
  • mediation by social services with agreement of the parties; failing that, either
    • arbitration, if agreeable to the parties, or
    • litigation.

11.12. When an adult member of a household withdraws, the disposition of any jointly-held property is determined as follows.

  • (a) The adult members of the household agree on terms in a written contract. [END]
    • If the adult members of the household cannot agree on terms:
      • The adult members of the household request arbitration by a court.
        • (b) The adult members of the household accept the terms set through arbitration. [END]
        • If the members do not accept the terms set through arbitration:
          • (c) The withdrawing member files suit against the household. The matter is resolved through the tort system. [END]

11.13. When the second-to-last remaining member of a household withdraws from the household, the household is automatically dissolved.

11.14. A household may be dissolved by an administrative procedure when the members mutually agree to dissolve it. The definition of "mutually agree" and the mechanism to arrive at such agreement are at the discretion of the adult members of the household.

11.15. A non-Resident who joins a household obtains Resident status, subject to a background check.

11.16. A household is limited to a maximum of four (4) adult members, except in the case when an individual is a minor member of the household at the time they reach the age of majority, and they elect to join the household.

11.17. A household opts in or out of optional tax-funded social services as a unit, and the election applies to each adult member of the household.

11.18. A non-Citizen member of a household is eligible to receive tax-funded benefit to which the household opts in, but does not obtain the right to vote, serve on juries, serve at a commissioned military rank, or work in government.

11.19. Limits on personal income apply to each member of a household individually.
