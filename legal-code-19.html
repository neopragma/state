Incarceration and Release

19. Healthcare.
This section of the legal code concerns the incarceration and release of convicted offenders.

19.2. Primacy of Human Rights and Legal Rights. All statutes in this section must be consistent with statutes pertaining to human rights and legal rights. Any statute in this section that violates human rights or legal rights will not be enforceable.

19.3. Early Release.

19.3.1. Special Circumstances. When special circumstances arise affecting an incarcerated individual, that individual or any other concerned party may petition a court for early release. The court may approve or deny the petition at its discretion.

19.3.2. Responsibility for Offenses During the Early Release Period. The released individual, the judge who approved the early release, and every individual involved in the petition for early release (including attorneys) will be liable for a share of damages for any offenses the individual commits between the date of early release and the originally-scheduled date of release.

19.3.3. Waiting Period After Release for Reinstatement of Full Citizenship. The waiting period for reinstatement of Full Citizenship begins with the actual release date, not the originally-scheduled release date.
