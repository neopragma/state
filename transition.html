Transition Plan

<= Previous | Next =>

The process of transforming the country from the United States to the American State is complicated by the depth and range of the changes as well as the need to effect the transition without disrupting the economy or interfering in the private lives of Americans to a greater extent than absolutely necessary. In addition, we must think about the international response to these changes, as hostile actors will be on the alert for opportunities to take advantage of any confusion, disorganization, or popular division in America during the transition.

The original version of the plan was created before the concept of the American State had been published. This was before we had the opportunity to receive feedback from interested parties, and before any serious objection or resistance to the change had emerged. It was meant as a starting point for planning the transition to the new system, based on issues we anticipate to be challenging, but was not based on actual experience of attempting to implement the changes. We expect the plan will change as we learn lessons through the process of carrying out the transition. Therefore, one should not think of the plan as rigid or final.
