3. Humanity and the Law
The Constitution is largely based on human rights and the State's duties to the people in that regard. This section of the legal code pertains to that subject.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom

3.1. An individual is human and therefore imbued with human rights and entitled to legal rights throughout the lifespan defined in the Constitution. This applies to all persons within the jurisdicton of the State regardless of Citizenship, Residency, or Visa status and regardless of criminal convictions.
CR: 5.1, 5.2, 5.3

3.2. Organizations of any kind, including corporations, non-profits, religions, labor unions, clubs, governmental entities, or any other type of organization not listed here, are not considered "human" for purposes of statutory law. Incorporation or registration of an organization will not serve as a legal shield to protect decision-makers in the organization from the legal consequences of their actions.
CR: 5.1.4
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 1.7.1, 1.7.3.

3.3. The Legal Code recognizes the human rights enumerated in the Constitution.
CR: 5.2
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom

3.3.1. When an individual is capable of upholding their own human rights, no other individual or organization may override that individual's choices regarding their own human rights.
CR: 5.1.2
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.1.

3.3.2. When an individual is not capable of upholding their own human rights, the decisions of their healthcare proxy or legal proxy have the same force as if the individual had made the decision under Statute 3.3.1. If no proxy is registered, then the responsibility falls to the nearest living relative who is mentally competent. If no such individual exists or they cannot be found or they explicitly refuse to serve as proxy, then the State will act as proxy.
CR: 5.1.3
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.1.

3.3.3. A woman who becomes pregnant and carries the pregnancy successfully to the point that the Constitution defines as the beginning of the human lifespan automatically becomes the healthcare and legal proxy for the unborn child. She remains the default and primary proxy for the child until the child reaches the age of majority. No administrative procedure is required to establish this relationship; however, a different proxy may be assigned should the woman prove to be an unfit parent, or should she choose to surrender this responsibility.
CR: 5.1.3
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.1.

3.3.3.1. Corollary: The natural father of the unborn child as described in Statute 3.3.3 shares responsibility for the child's welfare. Should the father fail to fulfill his duties to the child, then the mother or her legal proxy may ask the State to require child support payments beginning from the time of fetal viability and extending until the child's 16th birthday.

3.3.3.2. Corollary: Should the pregnancy be a result of (a) forcible rape or (b) abuse of a minor, then the natural father is not permitted to serve as the healthcare proxy or legal proxy of the child; however, he is still considered financially responsible under Statute 3.3.3.1.

3.3.4. Should a pregnancy end unsuccessfully subsequent to the point of viability by a natural process that was not caused by the woman's free choice of reckless action (such as consuming alcohol while pregnant), there is no violation of the human rights of the unborn child, regardless of the stage of development at the point the pregnancy ends. This provision applies to miscarriages and stillbirths.
CR: 5.1.2, 5.2.1
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.1.

3.3.5. An individual is free to make the choice to end their own life, or the life of one who for whom they are responsible as a healthcare proxy or legal proxy. In the latter case, an investigation is mandatory to verify the proxy exercised due diligence.
CR: 5.1.2, 5.2.1
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.1.

3.3.6. The State is prohibited from ending an individual's life through an administrative procedure of any kind.
CR: 5.2.1.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.1.

3.3.6.1. The death penalty is categorically prohibited.

3.3.6.2. Everyone is prohibited from ending anyone's life intentionally or through negligence.

3.3.6.3. Anyone who ends the life of a pregnant woman intentionally or through negligence will be liable for ending the lives of any viable unborn she carried at the time of the offense.

3.3.6.4. Unavoidable Accident

In the case of an unavoidable accident when neither intent nor negligence is proven, no one will be held liable for loss of life arising from the accident.

3.3.6.5. Medical Error

In carrying out their duties, if medical personnel can show they acted with due diligence they will not be held liable for the deaths of patients in their care. This applies to (a) doctors, nurses, and other professionals; (b) emergency responders; (c) individuals attempting to render aid. The designated standards-setting organization for medical practice is responsible for defining the term, due diligence. Regarding case (c), the test is whether the individual acted in a way a normal person would consider reasonable under the circumstances.

3.3.6.6. Health Proxy Decision

In carrying out their duties, if a health proxy can show they acted with due diligence they will not be charged when they make the decision to terminate the life of the individual under their care. An investigation is mandatory when a health proxy makes this decision.

3.3.6.7. Free Choice of Reckless Action

If an individual exercises their free choice of reckless action in a situation that may result in their death, then no one will be charged in their death. This applies to (a) law enforcement officers facing the imminent threat of violence, acting in self defense or in the defense of the public; (b) individuals facing the imminent threat of violence, acting in self defense or in defense of those around them; and (c) owners or operators of public or private facilities that are inherently dangerous and that have appropriate warnings posted.

3.3.6.8. Enemy Combatants

In circumstances of war or insurrection, it is lawful to kill those fighting on behalf of enemies of the State.

3.3.7. Everyone is prohibited from accosting, stalking, inducing fear into, or interfering in any way with anyone, physically or by means of technology. This offense is called assault.
CR: 5.2.3.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.2.

3.3.8. Everyone is prohibited from striking, grabbing, pushing, or touching anyone in any manner without their permission, with or without a weapon, or causing harm to anyone through technological means. This offense is called battery. Exceptions: Culturally-appropriate gestures such as tapping someone on the shoulder to get their attention or shaking hands when meeting someone or making an agreement.
CR: 5.2.2.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.2.

3.3.9. Individual liberty extends to any and all actions that do not interfere with other people's ability to exercise their own individual liberty. Everyone is prohibited from restricting anyone else's individual liberty. This is the Kindergarten Wisdom Statute: One's rights end where the next person's rights begin.
CR: 5.2.3.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.3.

3.3.10. Everyone is prohibited from creating a condition of bondage for others, such as employees, members of an organization, or members of a household. Bondage is a condition of life in which an individual cannot realistically act on choices to which they are entitled under the law. Examples include wage slavery, slavery, religious cults, kidnapping, abusive domestic relationships, and any other action that creates a condition of bondage.
CR: 5.2.4.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.4.

3.3.11. Everyone is prohibited from trespassing on anyone's private residential property without the permisson of the property owner. Exceptions: (a) law enforcement personnel acting on a search warrant; (b) emergency responders (or anyone else) addressing an urgent problem, such as a fire, gas leak, heart attack victim, etc.
CR: 5.2.5.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.5.

3.3.12. Visitors to private property or their survivors, whether invited or uninvited, who sustain injury or suffer death accidentally while on the property, and who are of majority age at the time, have no legal cause to file suit against the property owners for damages or for recovery of medical costs arising from their own actions. This statute does not apply to injury or death caused by the actions of the property owner.

3.3.13. Everyone is prohibited from entering anyone's residence, whether owned or rented, without the permisson of the resident. Exceptions: (a) law enforcement personnel acting on a search warrant; (b) emergency responders (or anyone else) addressing an urgent problem, such as a fire, gas leak, heart attack victim, etc.; (c) with respect to rental property, the property owner or their designated agent, to carry out lawful inspections or to perform maintenance; in this case, the resident must let them in, unless the resident is absent and does not respond to normal communication. Exception: Squatters occupying private property are not protected by this Statute.
CR: 5.2.5.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.5.

3.3.14. Everyone other than owners, employees, and contractors with active work orders is prohibited from trespassing on anyone's private commercial property except during posted business hours or to perform requested work. Exceptions: (a) law enforcement personnel acting on a search warrant; (b) emergency responders (or anyone else) addressing an urgent problem, such as a fire, gas leak, heart attack victim, etc.
CR: 5.2.5.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.5.

3.3.15. Everyone is prohibited from making a public call for violence against anyone, or for property damage. This offense is called incitement.
CR: 5.2.1.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.1.

3.3.16. Everyone is prohibited from creating a general mood of violence or hatred against anyone, even without naming the target individual or group. This offense is called stochastic terrorism.
CR: 5.2.1.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.1.

3.3.17. Everyone is prohibited from setting traps designed to cause harm automatically to trespassers or intruders. Guard dogs and electrified fences are not considered "automated traps" if warning signs are prominently posted; a trespasser who ignores easily-seen warnings will be considered to have exercised the free choice of reckless action. In the absence of warning signs, guard dogs and electrified fences are considered "automated traps" for purposes of this Statute. Any other form of automated trap is also covered under this Statute. A generic warning, such as "Warning: Booby Traps Inside!" will not circumvent the intent of this Statute. Warning signs may be placed at the normal entrances to the property. Should an intruder enter by some other route, such as climbing a back wall or breaking a window, they will be considered to have exercised the free choice of reckless action.
CR: 5.2.1.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 3.2.1.

3.3.18. Everyone is entitled to basic emergency healthcare, including individuals who are not Citizen or Residents, and Citizens who have opted out of the national healthcare system.
CR: [TBD]
BG: Backgrounder on Healthcare, Section 1.1.

3.3.18.1. Corollary: Neither (a) non-Citizens nor (b) Citizens who have opted out of the national healthcare system are entitled to extraordinary life-saving measures, if such measures would deny access to care by Citizens who have opted into the national healthcare system.

3.3.19. Everyone of majority age has the duty to protect, defend, and guide those of minority age.

3.3.19.1. Corollary: The punishment for any crime whose victim is a minor will be double the standard punishment.
