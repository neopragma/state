Citizenship

5. Citizenship
This section of the Legal Code concerns the definition of citizenship and the rights, privileges, and duties associated with citizenship.
CR: 5.6
BG: Backgrounder on Citizenship

5.1. Becoming a Citizen
CR: 5.6.3.

5.1.1. The natural child of a Citizen (mother or father) is a Full Citizen from the point of fetal viability. This includes a child carried by a surrogate mother on behalf of a Citizen.

5.1.2. The clone of a Citizen is a Full Citizen from the point of fetal viability.

5.1.3. A child of foreign origin who is adopted by a Citizen becomes a Provisional Citizen at the start of the adoption process. If the adoption process is not completed, the Provisional Citizenship is forfeited. If the adoption process is completed, the child becomes a Full Citizen.

5.1.4. A Resident who lives in America for a minimum of five (5) years with no criminal convictions may apply for Citizenship. Upon approval, the Resident becomes a Full Citizen by naturalization.

5.1.5. TR: A citizen of the United States of America who elects to remain in America through the transitional period automatically becomes a Full Citizen of America at the conclusion of the transitional period. Evidence of citizenship may be any of (a) an official birth certificate or a certified copy of the official birth certificate; (b) a United States passport, even if expired; (c) any credible evidence of residency and work in the United States.

5.2. Full Citizenship
CR: 5.6.1.

5.2.1. On acquiring Citizenship by any means provided in the law, a person becomes a Full Citizen.
CR: 5.6.1.1.

5.2.2. A Full Citizen has the duty to live within the law.
CR: 5.6.1.5.

5.2.3. A Full Citizen of majority age has the duty to cast a vote whenever a matter is brought before the people for a vote.
CR: 5.6.1.2.

5.2.4. A Full Citizen of majority age has the duty to serve on a jury whever they are called to do so.
CR: 5.6.1.3.

5.2.5. A Full Citizen of majority age has the duty to participate fully in the system by opting into all optional social services and taxes.
CR: 5.6.1.4.

5.2.6. A Full Citizen of majority age is eligible to serve in the Government, subject to other requirements of the position.
CR: 5.6.1.8.

5.2.7. A Full Citizen of majority age is eligible to serve in a law enforcement occupation, subject to other requirements of the position.
CR: 5.6.1.10.

5.2.8. A Full Citizen of majority is eligible to serve at a commissioned officer rank in the military, subject to other requirements of the position.
CR: 5.6.1.9.

5.2.9. One vote by a Full Citizen who holds a four-year university degree or has equivalent education carries the weight of three (3) points.
CR: 5.6.1.6.

5.2.10. One vote by a Full Citizen who does not hold a four-year university degree or has equivalent education carries the weight of two (2) points.
CR: 5.6.1.6.

5.2.11. The signature of a Full Citizen who holds a four-year university degree or has equivalent carries the weight of three (3) points on a petition to the Government.
CR: 5.6.1.7.

5.2.12. The signature of a Full Citizen who does not hold a four-year university degree or has equivalent education carries the weight of two (2) points on a petition to the Government.
CR: 5.6.1.7.

5.2.13. A Full Citizen must not hold citizenship in any foreign country.
CR: 5.6.2.1.

5.3. Basic Citizenship
CR: 5.6.2.

5.3.1. A Full Citizen becomes a Basic Citizen by exercising the free choice not to fulfill one or more of the duties of Full Citizenship stipulated under section 5.2, including 5.2.2 (criminal conviction), 5.2.3 (failure to cast a vote), 5.2.4 (failure to serve on a jury), 5.2.5 (opting out of one or more optional tax-supported social services), 5.2.13 (acquiring or failing to renounce citizenship in another country).
CR: 5.6.2.1.

5.3.2. A Full Citizen of minor age cannot choose Basic Citizenship, even by committing a crime.
CR: 5.6.2.1.

5.3.3. No one will be deprived of their Basic Citizenship for any reason, including their own renunciation of Citizenship. The State is prohibited from removing Basic Citizenship status from an individual regardless of criminal convictions.
CR: 5.6.2.8.

5.3.4. In the case that a Citizen must renounce their Citizenship in order to qualify for residency status in a foreign country, the State will verify such renunciation if requested by the foreign government, but will recognize the individual's Citizenship in perpetuity, and they may return to America at any time with their Citizenship intact.
CR: 5.6.2.8.

5.3.5. Anyone who chose Basic Citizenship by committing a crime may restore their Full Citizenship by completing their sentence and living within the law for the greater of (a) one calendar year, or (b) the period of time specified by the court at the time of sentencing; and maintaining all the other duties of Full Citizenship.
CR: 5.6.2.2.

5.3.6. Anyone who chose Basic Citizenship by failing to cast a vote in a matter that was brought before the people for a vote may restore their Full Citizenship by casting a vote in the next (or another) matter; and maintaining all the other duties of Full Citizenship.
CR: 5.6.2.2.

5.3.7. Anyone who chose Basic Citizenship by failing to serve on a jury may restore their Full Citizenship by serving on the jury the next time they are called to serve; and maintaining all the other duties of Full Citizenship.
CR: 5.6.2.2.

5.3.8. Anyone who chose Basic Citizenship by opting out of one or more tax-supported social services may restore their Full Citizenship by opting into all such services in the next (or any future) tax filing cycle; and maintaining all the other duties of Full Citizenship.
CR: 5.6.2.2.

5.3.9. Anyone who chose Basic Citizenship by acquiring or maintaining citizenship in another country may restore their Full Citizenship by showing evidence they have renounced such foreign citizenship; and maintaining all the other duties of Full Citizenship.
CR: 5.6.2.2.

5.3.10. A Basic Citizen has the duty to live within the law.
CR: 5.6.2.5.

5.3.11. A Basic Citizen of majority age has the right to cast a vote in all matters that are brought before the people for a vote.
CR: 5.6.2.3.

5.3.12. A Basic Citizen of majority age has the right to serve on a jury whenever called to do so.
CR: 5.6.2.4.

5.3.13. One vote by a Basic Citizen carries the weight of one (1) point.
CR: 5.6.2.6.

5.3.11. The signature of a Basic Citizen carries the weight of one (1) point on a petition to the Government.
CR: 5.6.2.7.
