Backgrounder on Simplification of Statutory Law

In our review of federal and state legal codes in the present-day US, to identify statutes we want to carry forward and to see models of legal phrasing, we noticed that many statutes are redundant. There may be a separate statute under each title or chapter in a state legal code prohibiting bribery of officials who work in the particular department covered by that title or chapter; only a single statute prohibiting bribery is necessary. There may be multiple statutes prohibiting the sale of specific controlled substances to minors; only a single such statute is necessary. There may be multiple statutes prohibiting frivilous lawsuits, each one specified under a different heading in the same legal code; only a single such statute is necessary. There are numerous examples of this pattern.

We will endeavor to preserve the useful parts of existing statutory law without duplicating the unnecessary complexity of present-day legal codes.
