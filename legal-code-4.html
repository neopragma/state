Privileges and Duties

4. Privileges and Duties
This section of the Legal Code concerns activities that are considered privileges rather than rights and the duties of those who wish to engage in privileged activities.
CR: 5.5
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Sections 5, 6.

4.1. Enterprises Supporting the Three Pillars of Independence
It is a privilege rather than a right to support the Three Pillars of Independence. The State is directed to establish the administrative apparatus, set standards, define procedures, and monitor compliance to ensure all enterprises that support the Three Pillars of Independence operate in a way consistent with the needs of the American people for sustainable freedom.
CR: 5.5.1
BG: Backgrounder on the Three Pillars of Independence, Sections 5, 6.

4.1.1. General Provisions

4.1.1.1. American Ownership
A minimum of 80% of ownership must be by American Citizens in any enterprise involved in any aspect of support for the Three Pillars of Independence.

4.1.1.2. American Base
All enterprises involved in any aspect of support for the Three Pillars of Independence must be based in America.

4.1.1.3. American Employment
A minimum of 80% of employees and contractors must be American Citizens in any enterprise involved in any aspect of support for the Three Pillars of Independence. Every working unit or team in such an enterprise must include at least one (1) American Citizen.

4.1.1.4. American Management
Every employee or contractor in any enterprise involved in any aspect of support for the Three Pillars of Independence who has direct reports must be a Full Citizen. Should their Citizenship change to Basic Citizenship for any reason, they must be removed from all roles that involve direct management of people until such time as they restore their Full Citizenship status.

4.1.1.5. Focus on Sustainability
The primary concern of any enterprise involved in any aspect of support for the Three Pillars of Independence must be the sustainable independence of the country as the basis for American freedom.

4.1.1.6. Infrastructure
All forms of national infrastructure are considered to be critical for the Three Pillars of Independence. Enterprises involved in infrastructure design, construction, maintenance, and support are subject to oversight and to the general provisions that apply to other enterprises that support the Three Pillars.

4.1.2. Food Pillar
All enterprises involved in agriculture, animal husbandry, fishing, or other forms of food production; all enterprises involved in food preparation and packaging; all enterprises involved in maintaining the national emergency food supply; and all enterprises involved in storing, transporting, and delivering foodstuffs and food products are considered to be supportive of the Food Pillar, unless 100% of their products are sold into foreign markets.

4.1.3. Energy Pillar
All enterprises involved in energy production and/or distribution, regardless of the raw materials, type of energy produced, or technologies used, are considered to be supportive of the Energy Pillar, unless 100% of their output is sold into foreign markets.

4.1.4. Defense Pillar
All enterprises involved in sourcing raw materials for the military, designing or manufacturing equipment for the military, developing and producing ammunition for the military, designing or manufacturing other supplies for the military, or providing training to military personnel, are considered to be supportive of the Defense Pillar.

4.2. Occupations with Societal Impact
The State is directed to establish the administrative apparatus, set standards, define procedures, and monitor compliance to ensure all occupations in which incompetent or fraudulent activity may have wide-ranging societal impact are governed by standards and oversight by qualified individuals. Occupations in this category will be determined and listed by the governmental operating Unit established by the State for this purpose. Standards and compliance rules will be managed by independent regulatory organizations in cooperation with the Government.
Note: Oversight is intentionally defined at the regulatory level rather than the statutory level to minimize the supporting bureaucracy and to simplify the process of modifying the standards and rules.
CR: 5.5.2

4.3. Ownership, Operation, and Maintenance of Dangerous Items
The State is directed to establish the administrative apparatus, set standards, define procedures, and monitor compliance to ensure the privileges of ownership, operation, and maintenance of items that are inherently dangerous even when used according to their intent and design (dangerous items) are governed by standards and oversight by qualified individuals. Items in this category will be determined and listed by the governmental operating Unit established by the State for this purpose. Standards, qualification, and licensure will be managed by independent standards-setting organizations in cooperation with the Government.
Note: Oversight is intentionally defined at the regulatory level rather than the statutory level to minimize the supporting bureaucracy and to simplify the process of modifying the standards and rules.
CR: 5.5.3
