Voting Procedures; Petition Procedures

7. Voting
This section of the Legal Code concerns the procedures to support voting by the general public. Procedures for voting within the National Advisory Board, the National Council, and the Legal Review Board are procedural matters for those organizations.
CR: 5.7
BG: Backgrounder on Citizenship

7.1. General Provisions

7.1.1. All Full Citizens have the duty to cast a vote in all matters that are brought before the public for a vote. Failure to fulfill this duty will incur penalties as described in Section 5 of the Legal Code.

7.1.2. All Basic Citizens have the right to cast a vote in all matters that are brought before the public for a vote, at their discretion.

7.1.3. The weight of a Citizen's vote is determined by the provisions of statutes in Section 5 of the Legal Code.

7.1.4. A criminal conviction does not affect a Citizen's right to vote.

7.1.5. Each Citizen who chooses to vote must cast exactly one (1) vote on each matter brought before the public for a vote. It is a Category 1 crime to vote more than once, as subversion of the governmental system undermines the Three Pillars of Independence.

7.1.6. Anyone who misrepresents themselves as another person in order to cast a vote under that person's name will be guilty of a Category 1 crime, as subversion of the governmental system undermines the Three Pillars of Independence.

7.1.7. Anyone who falsifies voting records in order to cast multiple votes will be guilty of a Category 1 crime, as subversion of the governmental system undermines the Three Pillars of Independence.

7.1.8. Anyone who falsifies, forges, destroys, alters, or conceals voting records in order to change the results of a referendum or an election will be guilty of a Category 1 crime, as subversion of the governmental system undermines the Three Pillars of Independence.

7.1.9. A Citizen's vote is a private matter. The State is directed to ensure all voting procedures and the handling of voter information and vote results protect the privacy of Citizens.

7.1.10. The results of a vote must be counted and published within two (2) calendar weeks of the date of the vote.

7.2. Voting and Technology

7.2.1. Voting procedures are to be supported by the appropriate level and kinds of technology available at the time of each referendum or election. The same referendum or election may be supported by multiple different technologies if local considerations make certain technologies unavailable in particular places. Regardless of the technologies used, they must support accuracy, fraud control, privacy, and timely reporting of results.

7.3. Ballots, Voting Weight, and Privacy

7.3.1. Citizens will be identified as Full Citizens or Basic Citizens in the State database of Citizens. The State is directed to ensure the database is maintained up to date.

7.3.2. When a Citizen casts a vote, they will be given a ballot that reflects the Citizen's voting weight in a manner that does not expose the Citizen's identity. The system must record the fact the Citizen has cast a vote but not associate the specific vote with the individual.

8. Petitioning
This section of the Legal Code concerns the procedures to support petitioning of the government by the general public.
CR: 5.7
BG: Backgrounder on Citizenship

8.1. General Provisions

8.1.1. All Citizens have the right to petition the government regarding any matter.

8.1.2. The weight of a Citizen's signature on a petition is determined by the provisions of statutes in Section 5 of the Legal Code.

8.1.3. A criminal conviction does not affect a Citizen's right to petition the government.

8.1.4. Each Citizen who chooses to sign a petition must sign exactly once. It is a Category 3 crime to sign more than once.

8.1.5. Anyone who misrepresents themselves as another person in order to sign a petition under that person's name will be guilty of a Category 1 crime, as subversion of the governmental system undermines the Three Pillars of Independence.

8.1.6. Anyone who falsifies petition records in order to register multiple signatures will be guilty of a Category 1 crime, as subversion of the governmental system undermines the Three Pillars of Independence.

8.1.7. Petitioning the government is a public matter. Petitions must be publicly accessible for anyone's review.

8.1.8. The petition registration system must apply the appropriate weight to each signature based on the Citizen data base as of the date of signing, not as of the date when the petition is started or when the government considers the petition.
