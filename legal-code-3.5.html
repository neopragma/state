Human Needs

3.5. Human Needs
This section pertains to the State's duties with respect to the human needs of the population. The State may be required by law to provide for a need directly, or may be directed to establish the conditions to enable the people to provide for the need, or a combination.
CR: 5.4, 4.3.5, 3.3.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.

3.5.1. Deficiency Needs
Deficiency Needs are those human needs without which a person cannot live as a human being but may only be able (or unable) to exist as an animal.
CR: 5.4.1.

3.5.1.1. Physiological Needs
This subsection concerns the duties of the State with respect to the people's physiological needs.
CR: 5.4.1.

3.5.1.1.1. Standards for Clean Air
The State is directed to establish standards for healthy, breathable air and establish mechanisms to monitor compliance with those standards.
CR: 5.4.1, 5.2.1.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.

3.5.1.1.2. Clean Air Entitlement
Neither the State nor any private sector entity may charge a fee for access to clean air.
CR: 5.4.1, 5.2.1, 4.1.3.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.

3.5.1.1.3. Standards for Potable Water
The State is directed to establish standards for healthy, potable water and establish mechanisms to monitor compliance with those standards.
CR: 5.4.1, 5.2.1, 3.3.1.
BG: Backgrounder on the Three Pillars of Independence
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.

3.5.1.1.4. Standards for Clean Water
The State is directed to establish standards for water to be used for industrial and agricultural purposes and for general washing, and establish mechanisms to monitor compliance with those standards.
CR: 5.4.1, 3.3.1.
BG: Backgrounder on the Three Pillars of Independence
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.

3.5.1.1.5. Fees for Potable and Clean Water
Private sector entities are authorized to charge fees to cover expenses and earn a profit for water-related services such as collection; filtering and processing; testing and inspection; bottling or other packaging; transport; storage; additives such as flouride, minerals, or vitamins; and maintaining pressurized water delivery systems compliant with standards established by the appropriate independent standards-setting organization(s) per Statutes 3.5.1.1.3 and 3.5.1.1.4. The public may protest fees that create an undue burden, and the Government reserves the right to cap such fees if private-sector entities persist in setting unreasonable fees.
CR: 5.4.1, 3.3.1.
BG: Backgrounder on the Three Pillars of Independence

3.5.1.1.6. Support for the Food Pillar
The State is directed to establish the administrative apparatus, set standards, and define procedures to support the Food Pillar of the Three Pillars of Independence, including food production, food quality, and food distribution infrastructure.
CR: 3.3.1, 4.3.5.
BG: Backgrounder on the Three Pillars of Independence
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.

3.5.1.1.7. Emergency Food Relief
The State is directed to establish the capacity and practical mechanisms to provide emergency food relief sufficient for five (5) million people for a period of three (3) months, and to maintain this capability in a state of readiness.
CR: 5.4.1.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.

3.5.1.1.8. Guaranteed Minimum Income
The State is directed to establish an optional, tax-funded program to provide guaranteed minimum income to Citizens who opt into the program. This is not conditional on financial need and not limited to any particular use by the Citizen. The rationale for the Statute on grounds of human needs is that destitute people are unable to live as human beings, and can only exist as animals. The rationale for the Statute on grounds of the Three Pillars of Independence is that destitute people are susceptible to external influences that may undermine the Three Pillars.
CR: 5.4.1.
BG: Backgrounder on the Three Pillars of Independence
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.

3.5.1.1.9. Housing Standards
The State is directed to establish quality standards and mechanisms for oversight and compliance enforcement for urban and suburban planning, construction, and habitability of residences.
CR: 5.4.1.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.

3.5.1.1.10. Low-Income Housing Support
The State is directed to establish mechanisms to assist low-income people in locating housing. These mechanisms may include tax incentives, subsidies, rent caps, and any other measures within the law and in consideration of the Light Touch principle.
CR: 5.4.1, 4.3.5.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.

3.5.1.1.11. Universal Healthcare
The State is directed to establish an optional, tax-funded program to provide healthcare services to Citizens who opt into the program. The program will be based on the single-payer model, in which services are provided by regulated private sector entities and the State makes all payments to service providers on behalf of the people.
CR: 5.4.1.
BG: Backgrounder on the Three Pillars of Independence
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.

3.5.1.2. Safety Needs
This subsection concerns the duties of the State with respect to the people's safety needs.
CR: 5.4.1.

3.5.1.2.1. Support for the Defense Pillar
The State is directed to establish the administrative apparatus, set standards, and define procedures to support the Defense Pillar of the Three Pillars of Independence, including all aspects of national defense and the infrastructure to support them.
CR: 3.3.3
BG: Backgrounder on the Three Pillars of Independence

3.5.1.2.2. Support for Law Enforcement
The State is directed to establish the administrative apparatus, set standards, and define procedures to support general law enforcement to defend the physical safety of the population, including (a) pursuit and arrest of suspects, (b) investigation of reported and suspected criminal activity, (c) intelligence-gathering on suspected criminal organizations, (d) rapid response to threat situations, (e) border control, and (f) prison management.
CR: 3.3.3
BG: Backgrounder on Crime and Punishment

3.5.1.2.2.1. Exclusion: Policing
Law enforcement operations will not include the active policing of the general population when no criminal activity has been reported and none is suspected.
CR: 4.2.1
BG: Backgrounder on Crime and Punishment

3.5.1.2.3. The State is directed to establish the administrative apparatus, set standards, define procedures, and monitor compliance to ensure the country's infrastructure is designed, built, and maintained to be robust and resilient, with considerations for locale-specific weather and geological phenomena and resistance to attack or sabotage.
CR: 3.3.3
BG: Backgrounder on the Three Pillars of Independence

3.5.1.2.4. The State is directed to encourage a wide range of entertainments for the population, through tax incentives, subsidies, support for school programs, and proactive outreach.
CR: 3.3.3
BG: Backgrounder on Bread and Circuses

3.5.1.3. The Need to Belong
This subsection concerns the duties of the State with respect to the people's need to belong.
CR: 5.4.1.

3.5.1.3.1. The State is directed to establish standards and support efforts to socialize Americans to cultivate a generally positive view of America and a sense of patriotism. This includes Citizens and Residents of the country, and must include special programs for adult immigrants.
This subsection concerns the duties of the State with respect to the people's need to belong.
CR: 5.4.1.
BG: Backgrounder on Education
BG: Backgrounder on Socialization and Civics

3.5.1.3.2. The State is directed to establish standards for basic education that include instruction on the structure and procedures of the Government and the Citizen's role in government.
This subsection concerns the duties of the State with respect to the people's need to belong.
CR: 5.4.1.
BG: Backgrounder on Education
BG: Backgrounder on Socialization and Civics

3.5.2. Growth Needs
Growth Needs are those human needs that enable individuals to pursue their goals in life and become "who they were meant to be." It is not possible for the State or any other external entity to provide growth needs directly, as an entitlement. Therefore, no law will be made that attempts to provide growth needs directly to the people. The intent of the State is to establish and maintain the conditions that enable people to pursue their own goals in life.
CR: 5.4.2.
BG: Backgrounder on Human Needs, Human Rights, Legal Rights, Privileges, Duties, and Freedom, Section 2.3.
BG: Backgrounder on the Purpose of the State.
